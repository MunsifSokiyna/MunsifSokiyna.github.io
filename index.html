About Me

I am a Ph.D. Candidate in Management Information Systems at the University of Alabama and part-time MIS instructor. My research explores sociotechnical dynamics in open-source software (OSS) communities, focusing on toxic behavior, governance, and the role of generative AI in shaping developer collaboration.

Research

  • Dissertation: Toxicity and Trust in OSS Communities: A Mixed-Methods Approach
  • In Review: OSS toxicity detection using large language models (LLMs)
  • In Progress: AI-mediated governance and trust in digital commons

Teaching

I teach Fundamentals of MIS (MIS 200) with 150+ students per semester. I focus on hands-on learning, real-world applications, and accessibility. My student evaluations average 4.23/5.
