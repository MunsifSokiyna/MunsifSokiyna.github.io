About Me.

Ph.D. Candidate in Information Systems | MIS Instructor | OSS & AI Researcher

I'm Munsif Sokiyna, a Ph.D. Candidate in Management Information Systems at the University of Alabama and a part-time instructor at the Culverhouse College of Business. My research focuses on toxic behavior and participation dynamics in open-source software (OSS) communities, with a special interest in how generative AI is reshaping collaboration, motivation, and governance.

I’m currently on the 2025–2026 academic job market. My dissertation examines toxic behavior in OSS communities using NLP and large language models (LLMs), alongside survey research and longitudinal analysis.

Beyond academia, I bring hands-on experience in web development, IT support, and team leadership from both corporate and freelance roles. I’ve taught courses in programming, systems analysis, and information systems, and I currently serve as a research assistant at the GRAIDE AI Business Lab.

My work has been published in journals such as Sustainable Computing and presented at conferences including AMCIS and the OpenForum Academy Symposium at Harvard. I’m passionate about fostering healthier digital communities and advancing the responsible use of AI in collaborative software environments.
